Assam Tea

Description

Assam tea is a black tea named after Assam, India, the region of its production. The Assam tea plant is indigenous to Assamâ€”initial efforts to plant the Chinese varieties in Assam soil did not succeed. Assam tea is now mostly grown at or near sea level and is known for its body, briskness, malty flavour, and strong, bright colour. Assam teas, or blends containing Assam tea, are often sold as "breakfast" teas. For instance, Irish breakfast tea, a maltier and stronger breakfast tea, consists of small-sized Assam tea leaves.

Ingredients

  • 2 tsp Assam Tea
  • 150 ml Water
  • Sugar (Optional)
  • Milk (Optional)

Steps

  1. Add 150 ml of water to a small pan and bring it to boil.
  2. Add 2 tsp of tea into the water and reduce the heat to low for 5 minutes.
  3. Strain the using a tea filter into a cup.
  4. Add sugar and milk to taste.
